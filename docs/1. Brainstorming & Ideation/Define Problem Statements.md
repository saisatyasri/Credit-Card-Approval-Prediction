# Define Problem Statements

**Project:** Credit Card Approval Prediction

---

## Problem Statement Formula

> "I am **[Customer]** / I'm trying to **[Goal]** / But **[Obstacle]** / Because **[Root Cause]** / Which makes me feel **[Emotion]**"

---

## Problem Statements

| PS No. | I am | I'm trying to | But | Because | Which makes me feel |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| PS-1 | A bank credit analyst reviewing applications daily | Evaluate each credit card application fairly and quickly | I have to manually check hundreds of applications every day | There is no automated system to pre-screen applicants based on financial risk | Overwhelmed, exhausted, and prone to making errors |
| PS-2 | A salaried employee applying for my first credit card | Know whether my application will be approved before I submit | I receive no feedback on my eligibility upfront | Banks do not provide instant eligibility checks — only post-submission decisions | Uncertain, anxious, and likely to apply to multiple banks unnecessarily |
| PS-3 | A financial compliance officer | Identify high-risk applicants with poor payment history | Many applicants with past-due records still slip through manual review | Payment history data is spread across thousands of records and hard to analyse manually | Concerned about regulatory risk and portfolio quality |

---

## Problem Summary

Banks and financial institutions face a dual challenge: **operational inefficiency** in reviewing thousands of applications and **risk of human error** in identifying high-risk applicants. Applicants, on the other hand, lack transparency into their approval likelihood, leading to unnecessary rejections and frustration. This project addresses both sides of the problem by providing an automated, data-driven prediction system accessible through a web interface.
